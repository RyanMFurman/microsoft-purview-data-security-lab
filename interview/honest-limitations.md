# Honest Limitations and Positioning

## Evidence statement

**DESIGNED FOR PURVIEW + IMPLEMENTED LOCALLY + SIMULATED INVESTIGATION**

No authorized Microsoft 365 tenant was available. The Microsoft 365 Developer Program sandbox request was not approved, and no employer, client, trial, or production tenant was authorized for this work.

## What I can claim

- I personally built and executed Python sensitive-data analysis against synthetic healthcare test cases.
- I personally validated configuration logic with PowerShell against a sanitized sample export.
- I created technically detailed Purview label, publishing, DLP, lifecycle, investigation, and Copilot-readiness designs based on official workflows.
- I developed and reasoned through a fictional forensic case.
- I translated technical risks into an executive summary, remediation plan, roadmap, and illustrative framework mapping.
- I understand the difference between technical design, deployment evidence, telemetry, exposure, and operating effectiveness.

## What I cannot claim

- Production or live-tenant Microsoft Purview administration.
- Deployment or validation of sensitivity labels, publishing policies, DLP, retention, eDiscovery, Audit, Insider Risk, Communication Compliance, DSPM for AI, or Copilot controls.
- Observation of real alerts, audit events, policy matches, investigations, AI prompts, users, repositories, or sensitive data.
- Proof that the local demonstration detector behaves like Microsoft Purview classifiers.
- HIPAA compliance, ISO certification, NIST conformance, legal sufficiency, or enterprise control maturity.

## Best interview answer

“My direct Purview work so far is a hands-on hybrid lab, not a production deployment. I was denied a developer sandbox and had no authorized tenant, so I chose not to fabricate access. I implemented and tested the parts I could defend locally, designed the tenant controls in implementation-level detail, and labeled the investigation as simulated. My prior Microsoft 365, IAM, healthcare-data, AI-evaluation, automation, and reporting experience transfers directly. In an authorized environment, I would start read-only, validate licensing and roles, establish a baseline, pilot in simulation, capture evidence, tune with owners, and only then progress enforcement.”

## If challenged about “hands-on”

“Hands-on means I wrote, ran, debugged, and tested the Python and PowerShell components and personally evaluated the test cases and policy decisions. It does not mean I clicked controls in a tenant. I make that boundary visible in the README and every major artifact.”

## Safe production transition

1. Obtain written tenant-owner authorization and confirm licensing.
2. Assign least-privileged reader roles and validate current Microsoft documentation and cmdlets.
3. Inventory labels, publishing policies, DLP, retention, audit configuration, repositories, groups, guests, and links.
4. Sanitize and secure baseline evidence.
5. Validate designs with synthetic content in a small role-diverse pilot.
6. Start DLP in simulation and measure precision, override use, alert routing, and analyst response.
7. Review access with data owners before granting Copilot pilot licenses.
8. Progress only through approved change control, rollback criteria, and measured outcomes.
